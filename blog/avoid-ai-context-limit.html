Hitting the Context Limit? How to Rescue a Long AI Chat

Every long AI conversation eventually gets sluggish, starts forgetting what you said an hour ago, or simply refuses to continue. That's the context limit showing up. Here's what's actually happening — and how to move the conversation somewhere with room before it stalls, without abandoning the thread.

The short version

  • Models can only "see" a fixed amount of conversation at once — the context window.
  • As a chat grows, the oldest turns fall out of view: the model starts forgetting, drifting, or stalling.
  • The fix is to continue the conversation in a fresh chat — ideally on a model with a roomy window — carrying the important context with you.
  • Continue AI captures the thread and hands it to a fresh ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini chat in one click, so you don't start over.

What the context limit is

An AI model reads your whole conversation every time it replies, but only up to a fixed size — its context window. Once the chat plus its answer would exceed that window, something has to give: the app trims or summarizes the oldest messages so the newest ones still fit. The model isn't broken; it just can't see the beginning of the thread anymore.

The signs you're hitting it

  • It forgets. Constraints you set early — a name, a format, a rule — quietly stop being honored.
  • It drifts. Answers get more generic, as if it's lost the specifics of your project.
  • It slows down. Very long threads get noticeably laggy to load and respond.
  • It refuses. Some apps surface an explicit "this conversation is too long" message and stop letting you continue.
A long chat doesn't fail all at once — it fades. By the time it "forgets," you've usually already lost a few answers to drift. Move it before it stalls, not after.

Why "just start a new chat" isn't enough

A fresh chat fixes the window, but a blank one throws away everything you built. So people copy-paste the old thread in — and run straight into the other problem: long conversations are only partly rendered on screen at a time, so a manual copy usually misses messages, and the formatting and code don't survive the trip. You end up with a new chat that's almost caught up, which is its own kind of frustrating.

FAQ

Why does my AI chat start forgetting things?

Because the conversation has grown past the model's context window. To fit your newest messages, the app trims or summarizes the oldest ones — so earlier details stop influencing the answers.

Can I keep the conversation when I start a fresh chat?

Yes — instead of copy-pasting, capture the thread and hand it to a new chat as a portable prompt, so the fresh chat continues with the context instead of a blank slate.
