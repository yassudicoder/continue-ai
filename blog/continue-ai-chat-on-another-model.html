How to Continue an AI Chat on Another Model — Without Losing Context

You're deep in a conversation with one AI and you want a second opinion, a roomier context window, or just a different model's strengths. The instinct is to copy-paste the whole thread into the new chat — and that's exactly where the context goes to die. Here's the faster, cleaner way to continue the conversation on another model with the thread intact.

The short version

  • Copy-pasting a long chat loses formatting, code blocks, and usually half the thread.
  • Instead, capture the whole conversation and hand it to ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini as a portable prompt — in one click.
  • The new model starts with the full context, so you continue instead of re-explaining.
  • It all happens on your device — no account, no servers, nothing transmitted.

Why you'd switch models mid-conversation

No single model wins at everything, and you usually only discover the mismatch halfway through a chat. Common reasons people move a conversation:

  • A second opinion. You've got a plan from one model and want another to pressure-test it.
  • The chat hit a wall. Long conversations slow down, start "forgetting" earlier messages, or refuse to continue. (More on that in rescuing a long chat.)
  • Different strengths. One model is better at code, another at long-document reasoning, another at quick drafts.
  • Availability. One service is down, rate-limited, or you've used up a quota.

The problem with copy-paste

Manually moving a conversation is worse than it looks. You select dozens of messages, paste them into the new model, and immediately lose the structure: who said what blurs together, code blocks flatten into plain text, and long threads get truncated because selecting them all is a chore. Then you spend the first few messages re-establishing context the model can't see. It's slow, lossy, and error-prone — exactly the busywork you switched models to avoid.

The point of switching models is to keep thinking — not to spend ten minutes re-typing what you already said. Carry the thread, not just the last message.

The one-click way: capture → package → hand off

Continue AI does the move for you in three steps, all on your machine:

  • Capture. It reads the conversation that's open — every user and assistant turn, including code — straight from the page.
  • Package. It builds a single portable prompt that carries the thread as context, with a clear instruction to pick up where you left off.
  • Hand off. It opens your chosen model — ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini — and drops the prompt in, ready to send.

You go from "I wish I could ask a different model" to actually asking it, with the full conversation behind it, in a click.

What "without losing context" actually means

"Context" isn't just your last question — it's the whole arc: the constraints you set early, the code that evolved, the decisions you made together. Continue AI carries that arc into the new chat, so the receiving model isn't guessing. The transfer is plain text you can read before you send it, so nothing happens behind your back.

It works on long, messy chats too

The hardest conversations to move by hand are the long ones — and those are exactly the ones where switching models helps most. Modern chat apps only keep part of a long thread on screen at a time (they "virtualize" it), which is why a manual copy so often misses messages. Continue AI is built to grab the full thread even on these long, virtualized chats, so the handoff isn't silently missing the middle.

Or just keep a copy

Sometimes you don't want to move the chat — you want to keep it. Continue AI can export any conversation as a PDF, an HTML page, an image, Markdown, or JSON, so you can archive a good session, share it, or drop it into your notes.

FAQ

Can I switch AI models without losing my conversation?

Yes. Instead of copy-pasting, you capture the whole chat and hand it to another model as a portable prompt, so the new model picks up with the full context — the messages, code and files from the original thread.

Which models can I move a conversation between?

ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. You can capture on any of the three and continue on any of the three.

No. Capturing and packaging happen on your device. There are no servers and nothing is transmitted to us. The conversation only goes to the AI you choose, when you choose to transfer it.
