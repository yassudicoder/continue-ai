How to Move a ChatGPT Conversation to Claude

You started a thread in ChatGPT, and now you want Claude's read on it — maybe for longer-document reasoning, a different writing voice, or a second opinion on an approach. Re-typing the whole thing is a waste. Here's how to carry the entire conversation from ChatGPT into Claude, code and context included.

The short version

  • Open the ChatGPT conversation you want to move.
  • Capture it, choose Claude as the destination, and transfer — the thread is packaged into a portable prompt and copied to your clipboard, ready to paste into a fresh Claude chat.
  • Claude continues with the full context instead of asking you to re-explain.
  • It's local-first: nothing is uploaded to any server of ours.

Why move a thread to Claude

People reach for Claude mid-conversation for a few recurring reasons: it's strong on long-document and codebase reasoning, it has a distinct writing voice that's useful for a second draft, and a fresh chat gives you a roomy context window when your ChatGPT thread is getting heavy. Whatever the reason, the friction is the same — you don't want to lose the conversation you've already built.

The manual way (and why it's painful)

By hand, you'd scroll the ChatGPT thread, select everything, paste it into Claude, and then spend a couple of messages explaining what it is and what you want next. Long threads make it worse: ChatGPT only keeps part of a long conversation rendered at once, so a naive select-all quietly misses messages, and code blocks lose their formatting on the way over.

The one-click way

With Continue AI installed, the move is a single action:

  • 1. Open the ChatGPT chat. The one you want Claude to continue.
  • 2. Capture it. Continue AI reads the full thread from the page — every turn, including code — and flags the capture if a long chat is hiding part of itself, so nothing goes missing silently.
  • 3. Choose Claude and copy. The conversation is packaged into a portable prompt and copied to your clipboard — open a new Claude chat, paste, and send.

Once you paste, Claude picks up already knowing the conversation — so your next message is the actual next question, not a recap.

✅

It works both ways. You can just as easily move a Claude thread back to ChatGPT, or send either one to Gemini — capture on any of the three, continue on any of the three.

What carries over

The handoff brings the whole arc of the conversation: the earlier turns that set up the problem, the code and snippets you iterated on, and a clear instruction for Claude to continue from your last message. Because the packaged prompt is plain text, you can read exactly what's being sent before you hit send — there's no hidden step.

Switching to Claude shouldn't cost you the thread you built in ChatGPT. Bring the conversation with you.

Is this private?

Yes. Capturing and packaging the ChatGPT conversation happen on your device — there are no servers of ours and nothing is transmitted to us, with no account or telemetry. The conversation only travels when you hand it to Claude, and then it goes to Claude (under Anthropic's privacy terms), not through us.

FAQ

Will Claude have the full ChatGPT conversation?

Yes — the transfer packages the captured thread (messages and code) into the prompt you paste into Claude, so it continues with that context rather than starting blank. If a long chat hides part of itself from the capture, Continue AI flags it rather than dropping it silently.

Can I go the other direction, Claude → ChatGPT?

Yes. Continue AI captures on ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini and hands off to any of them, in either direction.

Does it work if the ChatGPT chat is really long?

Yes — it's built to grab the full thread even on long, virtualized chats where the page only keeps part of the conversation on screen at once, and it flags the capture if it can't reach everything, so nothing goes missing silently.
